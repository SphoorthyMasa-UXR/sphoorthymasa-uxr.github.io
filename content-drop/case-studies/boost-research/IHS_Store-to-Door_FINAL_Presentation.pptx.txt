Executive Summary
OBJECTIVE: Validate demand for Boost Mobile's Store-to-Door in-home setup service across 5 age segments.

S2D interest is driven by CONVENIENCE + SWITCH INTENT, not by tech inability.
  • 18-34: Highest interest (3.31/5), highest switch intent, highest NPS (7.7/10)
  • 65+: Lowest interest (2.13/5), structural trust barriers, not in-market
  • Difference is statistically significant: t=7.22, p<0.001 

HYPOTHESES TESTED:
  H1: 'Older users who struggle with tech will prefer S2D' → DEBUNKED (correlation ≈ 0)
  H2: 'Rural users prefer S2D due to no store access' → NOT SUPPORTED (p=0.12)
  H3: 'Hassle-free switching appeals to active switchers' → CONFIRMED (p<0.001)

BUSINESS IMPLICATION: Focus 70%+ of S2D budget on 18-44. Deprioritize 55+.
"Let me give you the exec summary upfront so you have the frame as we go deeper. The big finding is counterintuitive: S2D interest is driven by convenience and switching intent, not by tech inability. The people who are most excited about this service are young, tech-savvy, and already shopping for a new carrier. The people who struggle most with phones — the 55+ crowd — actually don't want this. We also tested two specific hypotheses that the team had going in. One was confirmed, one was debunked. I'll walk through both with the data for a deeper dive."

Methodology & Analysis Rigor
STUDY DESIGN
  • Unmoderated quant survey (UserZoom)
  • 5 parallel age segments
  • N = 539 completed responses
  • US adults, active mobile users
  • Non-Boost customers (prospect audience)
  • Concept exposure: S2D screenshots
  • Data: June 17-18, 2026

SAMPLE BY SEGMENT
  • 18-34: n=102
  • 35-44: n=105
  • 45-54: n=103
  • 55-64: n=110
  • 65+: n=119
ANALYSIS METHODS
  • Descriptive: means, %, distributions
  • Top-2-Box (T2B): % scoring 4-5 on 5pt scale
  • 95% Confidence Intervals on all key metrics
  • Independent-samples t-tests (group comparisons)
  • Pearson r correlations (variable relationships)
  • Cross-tabulation: Area × S2D metrics
  • Within-group segmentation (e.g., conf level × interest)

STATISTICAL REPORTING
  • *** p < 0.001
  • ** p < 0.01
  • * p < 0.05
  • NS = not significant (p ≥ 0.05)
  • All data read programmatically from Excel
  • Zero manual transcription

"Alright, let's get into the insights in priority order. Number one: 18-34 is unambiguously our target audience. They lead on every single metric. Interest is 3.31 out of 5, nearly half score 4 or 5. Switch intent is highest — 29% are likely to switch in the next year. And the difference versus 65+ is massive and statistically significant at p less than 0.001. Insight #2: The Confidence Paradox — Explained
FINDING:High confidence does NOT mean low S2D interest.Correlations are near zero within every age group:  18-34: r = 0.078  35-44: r = -0.078  45-54: r = -0.164  55-64: r = 0.044  65+:   r = -0.128The REAL driver: convenience appetite.Confident 18-34 hassle-free: 3.21/5Confident 65+ hassle-free: 1.95/5  p < 0.001 ***SO WHAT:Position S2D as TIME-SAVING convenience (DoorDash),NOT as 'help for the incompetent' (tech support).
"This one surprises people. You'd think that if someone is highly confident setting up their phone, they wouldn't want S2D. But look at these correlations — they're all near zero. Confidence simply does not predict interest. What DOES predict it? Convenience appetite. When we isolate just the highly confident users and ask about hassle-free appeal, 18-34 scores 3.21 versus 1.95 for 65+, and that difference is significant at p less than 0.001. The takeaway for positioning: this is DoorDash for phones, not tech support. Don't message it as help — message it as 'save time.'"

Insight #3: Switch Intent Is the Real Predictor
FINDING:S2D interest tracks with switching intent:  18-34: 29% likely to switch → 47% S2D interested  65+: 8% likely to switch → 14% S2D interested  Young vs Old switch: t=7.52, p<0.001 ***If you're not shopping, S2D is irrelevant — regardlessof whether you need help with phones.SO WHAT:Target in-market users (carrier comparison sites,'thinking about switching' retargeting). S2D convertsactive shoppers — it doesn't create new switching intent.
"Here's the underlying mechanism. S2D interest tracks almost perfectly with switching intent. 29% of 18-34 are likely to switch and 47% are interested in S2D. Only 7% of 65+ are likely to switch and only 14% care about S2D. If you're not shopping for a new carrier, S2D is irrelevant — no matter how bad you are with phones. The implication: target people who are already in-market. Retarget on carrier comparison sites. Don't try to create switching intent from scratch — just be there when someone's already considering it."

Insight #4: Rural Hypothesis — DEBUNKED
ASSUMPTION:'No nearby Boost stores → prefer home service.'REALITY (from data):  Urban (n=151): interest = 2.80/5  Suburban (n=279): interest = 2.69/5  Rural (n=109): interest = 2.54/5  T-test: t=-1.55, p=0.123 (NS)Urban users actually trend HIGHER.SO WHAT:Do NOT prioritize rural-first geographic rollout.Target by AGE (18-44), not by store proximity.Urban/suburban 18-44 is the sweet spot.
"Now this one we went in assuming would be true — that rural users without nearby stores would love home service. The data says no. Urban users actually trend slightly higher on S2D interest — 2.80 versus 2.54 for rural. The t-test gives us p equals 0.12, not significant. Why? Because rural respondents in our sample skew older, and older means more trust barriers plus lower switch intent. So the geographic strategy should follow age demographics, not store density. Urban and suburban 18-44 is the sweet spot."

Insight #5: 'Free Delivery + Free Setup' Wins Message Testing
FINDING:'Free delivery + free setup. Just pick a time.'is the top-performing message across ages.Why it wins:  • 'Free' addresses hidden-cost concern  • 'Pick a time' gives control/flexibility  • Tangible & specific (not vague)Weakest: 'Your personal phone expert'  — feels sales-y, no specificitySO WHAT:Use as primary campaign headline.A/B test vs 'Switch without stress' for young adults.Kill 'personal phone expert' from all creative.
"We tested six different S2D taglines. The winner across most age groups is 'Free delivery plus free setup. Just pick a time that works.' It wins because it hits two things simultaneously — it says free, which addresses the hidden-cost concern, and it says pick a time, which gives people control. The weakest performer was 'Your personal phone expert' — it feels vague and sales-y. Recommendation: use the winning message as primary campaign copy and kill the 'personal expert' language from all creative."

Insight #7: Trust Barriers Are Structural for 55+
FINDING:Key home-visit barriers increase with age:These are NOT solvable with messaging alone.'Prefer self-setup' is high across all ages —but combined with 'stranger' + 'data trust' + 'prefer store',55+ has compounding barriers that messaging can't fix.Qualitative evidence (verbatim):  • 'Nothing would make me choose this' (65+)  • 'I don't want strangers in my home' (55-64)  • 'For security reasons, no' (55-64)SO WHAT:Do NOT invest S2D marketing in 55+.Offer video-call guided setup as alternative.Serve this segment through stores + phone support.
"Last major insight. For 55+, the barriers to S2D are structural — meaning they can't be solved with better messaging. 'Stranger in my home,' 'don't trust someone with my passwords,' 'I'd prefer to go to a store' — these compound. And the qualitative responses confirm it: people literally saying 'nothing would make me choose this.' These aren't objections you overcome with a better tagline. The recommendation: don't force S2D on 55+. Offer video-call guided setup as an alternative product that addresses the help need without the home-visit discomfort."

Actionable Recommendations
1. BUDGET: Allocate 70%+ of S2D marketing spend to ages 18-44 (digital: social, search, YouTube)
   → Data shows 3-5x higher engagement metrics vs 55+. Every $ has higher conversion potential.

2. POSITIONING: Frame S2D as convenience/time-saving — NOT as 'help for the tech-challenged'
   → Confident users want it for ease. Think DoorDash positioning, not tech support positioning.

3. MESSAGING: Lead with 'Free delivery + free setup. Just pick a time that works.'
   → Top-performing message in testing. Combine with coverage proof ('Powered by T-Mobile 5G').

4. TARGETING: Focus on users actively researching carrier switches (retargeting, comparison sites)
   → S2D converts active shoppers. It doesn't create switching intent from scratch.

5. NETWORK PROOF: Always pair S2D with coverage credibility
   → 'Worse coverage' is the #1 universal concern. S2D alone doesn't overcome this.

6. REFERRAL: Build post-appointment referral program for 18-34 (NPS potential is highest)
   → Each satisfied customer becomes an acquisition channel.

7. ALTERNATIVE: Offer video-call guided setup for 45+ who want help but NOT home visits
   → Addresses trust barriers while still capturing demand for assistance.

8. GEOGRAPHIC: Target by age demographics, NOT store density. Rural ≠ higher S2D demand.
"Alright, pulling it all together into what we should actually do. Eight recommendations in priority order. I won't read all of them but the top three: budget allocation to 18-44 through digital channels, positioning as convenience not help, and leading with the winning message. The one I want to emphasize most: always pair S2D messaging with network coverage proof. Coverage is the universal concern — S2D alone isn't enough to overcome it."
