Business
Problem 


High Drop-Off at IMEI Step

Significant bounce on IMEI entry page despite the redesign to simplify instructions.
Critical step for BYOD activation funnel.
Drop-off = lost activations and revenue.




Why are users bouncing at IMEI step?
Do users understand:
What IMEI is
How to find it
Why it is required
What gaps are causing hesitation or drop-off

Research Objectives

Quantitative survey (n=1000) via UserTesting
Unmoderated usability (n=15) via UserZoom
Executed in parallel
Executed timeline: 3 days

Methodology Overview

Survey:
IMEI understanding
Instructions clarity
Privacy/security perception
Usability Test:
Visit live Boost BYOD flow
Locate IMEI on their device
Enter IMEI and proceed

Study Design

Key Insights

01 - Users Don’t Understand What IMEI Is
Low baseline awareness of IMEI concept
Users hesitant to proceed without understanding purpose
Creates cognitive friction before action

02 - IMEI Is Perceived as Low Importance and Low Comfort to Share
Importance of IMEI

02 
IMEI ranked lowest among information users consider important during purchase 
IMEI ranked lowest in comfort level for sharing personal/device information 
Indicates low perceived relevance and higher sensitivity 

User Comfort Level

03 - Trust & Privacy Concerns Block Completion
Users unsure why IMEI is needed
Concern about sharing device-specific information
No reassurance or explanation present


04 - Navigation Mismatch Breaks User Expectations 
IMEI help link redirects to generic BYOD page
No IMEI-specific content on landing
Users expect dedicated IMEI explanation


05 - Instruction Clarity Is Strong, But Purpose and Trust Remain Weak 

06 - Users Feel Confident Finding IMEI When Guided 
81% reported confidence in finding IMEI after viewing instructions 
56.4% of users said they’d be willing to enter IMEI if “why it’s needed” is clearly explained 
Indicates instructions are directionally effective
