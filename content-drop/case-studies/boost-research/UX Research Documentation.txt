Jira Ticket Description (optional)
Work with Dave to get the designs for the Affirm that is part of the Affirm Phase 1 to capture UX feedback. This needs to be the flow understanding from PLP to doing the credit check, and after.

The purpose of the research is to inform potential updates to the Affirm Phase 1 post launch, as well as to help the UX and Product make decisions for phase 2.
Research Goals 
To assess the clarity, trust, and usability of the Affirm financing option within the device purchase journey, specifically identifying friction points during the transition between the Boost and Affirm interfaces
Research Objectives 
Evaluate how clearly shoppers understand the Affirm financing option when purchasing a device and identify points of confusion across the purchase journey, particularly around:
* Multiple price presentations on the Product Listing Page (PLP)
* The “Ways to Buy” options on the Product Detail Page (PDP)
* User expectations and understanding of the Affirm financing flow
* Transition between Boost checkout → Affirm site → Boost confirmation
* Whether the “Special Deal” option distracts or misleads users compared to financing
Methodology 
Suggested moderated usability testing . Because of the time restraints, we will proceed with unmoderated usability testing
Target Participants(N=5)
* Gen pop
* Users who have previously used financing services (Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay)
* Individuals looking to upgrade their smartphone within the next 3-6 months.
* Comfortable shopping for electronics online

Research Goal
Understand how willing users are to provide a credit card when starting a free trial and identify the concerns, conditions, and reassurances that influence that decision.
Research Objectives
 • Measure willingness to enter a credit card to access a free trial
• Identify major barriers to credit card entry such as trust, fear of automatic charges, or cancellation concerns
• Identify messages or safeguards that increase comfort with entering payment information
• Understand preferred free trial duration
• Understand whether users prefer trials for a single device or across multiple devices/household
• Understand what users evaluate during a free trial before deciding whether to continue the service
Methodology

• Approximately 100 participants
• Multiple-choice and multi-select questions with one open-ended question
• Logic branching based on previous responses
• Insights focused on themes and patterns rather than statistical significance

• Smartphone users

Research Objectives & Goals
                                                                                    * Primary Objective: Determine which free-trial entry flow (Payment Required vs. No Payment) yields a better balance of user trust and conversion intent.
                                                                                    * Secondary Objective: Identify friction points and security concerns associated with providing payment details for a "free" service.
                                                                                    * Success Metric: Measure the Preference Rate, Task Completion Time, and Trust Score across both flows.


Methodology
Unmoderated Usability Testing & Preference Test
                                                                                    * This methodology allows for a side-by-side comparison of two distinct flows (A/B testing) in a natural environment, providing both behavioral data (task success) and attitudinal data (preference).
                                                                                    * Used only as a post-task follow-up to quantify preference and perceived value.


Target Participants
                                                                                    * Profile: "Switchers" or "Contract-Free" Seekers.
                                                                                    * Demographics: Adults (18–55) who currently pay for their own mobile service.

✅ Paid Media / Landing Page
 
Paid Media / Landing Page 
Sphoorthy Masa


Requester: Kit Hackett
Date: 7/25/25


Research Goals


What are you trying to learn?


Understand what customers want to see on a landing page when they click on a paid media ad for each funnel stage; upper funnel /awareness, mid funnel /consideration, and low funnel /conversion. 


                                                                                    * Understand User Expectations: Determine what information users anticipate seeing on a landing page based on the ad they clicked (e.g., a specific phone deal vs. a general plan offer).
                                                                                    * Identify Key Content: Pinpoint the essential pieces of information (e.g., price, data limits, contract terms, phone specifications) that give users the confidence to make a purchase decision.
                                                                                    * Evaluate Ad-to-Page Cohesion: Assess whether the landing pages effectively deliver on the promise made in the advertisements.
                                                                                    * Uncover Friction Points: Identify specific usability issues, confusing content, or missing information on the landing pages that may cause users to abandon the page.
                                                                                    * Inform Design Strategy: Provide actionable recommendations to improve landing page layout, content hierarchy, and overall user experience for different funnel stages.


Scope/Hypothesis


If we can learn what customers want to see on a landing page from clicking an ad, we can build more insightful landing pages, which will lower bounce rate and increase conversions. 


Success Metrics & Completion (Optional)
Bounce Rate 
Conversion Rate 


Research Methodology
Phase 1: Surveys - Affordable Plan, iPhone 16e
Phase 2: Unmoderated usability testing (run parallely with the surveys)
Phase 3: Moderated Interviews (if deep dive is needed)


Questions for Participants - PM/PD


When you clicked on this paid media ad, where were you thinking it would direct you? 
When you visit a paid media landing page, what content do you want to see on the page? 
When you saw this paid media ad, did it make you want to click on it? Does it have a good hook? 
When you go to the landing page, does it give you enough information to feel confident to make a purchase?

✅ PDP Page Research Plan
PDP Page Research Plan
Sphoorthy Masa, Dec’25


1. Research Objectives
                                                                                    * Evaluate Comprehension of Payment Options: Determine if users clearly understand the differences between Special Deal Price, 36-Month Financing, and Pay Today.
                                                                                    * Validate Service Requirement Logic: Assess if users understand the condition that choosing promotional pricing or financing requires switching to Boost Mobile, whereas Pay Today may offer different flexibility.
                                                                                    * Identify Friction Points: Uncover confusing elements when toggling between payment and service options.
                                                                                    * Assess Add to Cart Expectations: Learn what users expect to happen immediately after making their selection.
                                                                                    * Measure Confidence: Determine if the page provides enough information for users to confidently proceed to checkout.

                                                                                    * Sample Size: 10 Participants
                                                                                    * Demographics:
                                                                                    * Gen Pop (National Audience)
                                                                                    * Age: 18–65
                                                                                    * Criteria/Screener: Currently owns a smartphone; responsible for their own mobile phone bill; open to switching carriers or buying a new phone in the next 6-12 months.
                                                                                    * Exclusions: Current employees of telecom providers.

BYOD Activation
BYOD Activation
Sphoorthy Masa - Mar’26


Background & Problem Statement
Boost Mobile is experiencing high churn within the first 60–90 days of prepaid BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) customers. Additionally, there are instances where SIMs/eSIMs are successfully shipped or delivered but never activated, resulting in lost customers before they experience the service.
This indicates a critical breakdown in the onboarding journey between:
Purchase → Activation → First successful use
The activation experience functions as a second conversion funnel, and current friction within this stage is likely contributing to:
                                                                                                                                                            * Early churn
                                                                                                                                                            * Revenue leakage
                                                                                                                                                            * Increased customer support costs
Research Goals
Primary Goals
                                                                                                                                                            * Identify friction points in the BYOD activation process
                                                                                                                                                            * Understand why users fail, abandon, or delay activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Evaluate whether the recently launched Unified Activation experience has reduced friction
Secondary Goals
                                                                                                                                                            * Identify misinterpretation of instructions and user expectations
                                                                                                                                                            * Distinguish between:
                                                                                                                                                            * UX/usability issues
                                                                                                                                                            * Technical/system issues
                                                                                                                                                            * Communication clarity gaps
Business Context & Metrics
To ground the research in business impact, the following baseline metrics will be analyzed:
Activation Funnel Metrics
                                                                                                                                                            * % of users who purchase → attempt activation
                                                                                                                                                            * % of users who start → complete activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Step-level drop-off rates
                                                                                                                                                            * Time to activation (TTA)
Failure Indicators
                                                                                                                                                            * % of SIMs/eSIMs never activated
                                                                                                                                                            * Activation retry rates
                                                                                                                                                            * Error rates across steps
Customer Support Signals
                                                                                                                                                            * Volume of activation-related calls/chats
                                                                                                                                                            * Top call drivers (e.g., porting issues, SIM issues, confusion in steps)
Churn Metrics
                                                                                                                                                            * 30 / 60 / 90-day churn rates
                                                                                                                                                            * Churn segmented by:
                                                                                                                                                            * Activated vs non-activated users
                                                                                                                                                            * Successful vs failed activation attempts
Key Research Questions
Behavioral
                                                                                                                                                            * At which steps do users drop off during activation?
                                                                                                                                                            * What actions lead to repeated attempts or abandonment?
Cognitive
                                                                                                                                                            * How do users interpret each step of the activation process?
                                                                                                                                                            * Where do expectations diverge from actual experience?
Comparative
                                                                                                                                                            * How does Unified Activation perform compared to legacy flows?
                                                                                                                                                            * Which flows still show high friction and why?
Operational
                                                                                                                                                            * What are the primary triggers for customer support interactions?
                                                                                                                                                            * What issues could be prevented through improved experience design?
Scope & Focus
                                                                                                                                                            * Focus exclusively on BYOD prepaid service activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Emphasis on digital activation experience (eSIM and online flows)
                                                                                                                                                            * Excludes:
                                                                                                                                                            * In-store activation experiences
                                                                                                                                                            * Device purchase journeys
This ensures alignment with the digital product experience domain and removes dependency on physical retail environments.
Methodology
A mixed-method research approach will be used to combine behavioral data with user insights.
Phase 1: Quantitative Analysis (Foundational)
Objective: Identify high-friction points and prioritize research areas
Inputs:
                                                                                                                                                            * Activation funnel analytics
                                                                                                                                                            * Event tracking data
                                                                                                                                                            * Customer support logs
                                                                                                                                                            * Churn data
Outputs:
                                                                                                                                                            * Drop-off points across activation steps
                                                                                                                                                            * High-impact failure points
                                                                                                                                                            * Segmentation of users (e.g., iOS vs Android, port-in vs new number)
                                                                                                                                                            * Identification of key activation flows to study
Phase 2: Moderated Interviews (Remote)
Objective: Understand user expectations, interpretations, and perceived barriers
Sample:
                                                                                                                                                            * 12–20 participants
                                                                                                                                                            * Mix of:
                                                                                                                                                            * Users who successfully activated
                                                                                                                                                            * Users who failed activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Users who did not attempt activation
Focus Areas:
                                                                                                                                                            * Expectations before starting activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Interpretation of instructions and steps
                                                                                                                                                            * Points of confusion or hesitation
                                                                                                                                                            * Perceived effort and trust in the process
Phase 3: Moderated Usability Testing (Remote eSIM Activation)
Objective: Observe real-time behavior and identify usability issues
Approach:
                                                                                                                                                            * Use eSIM activation to enable remote testing
                                                                                                                                                            * Participants complete activation via screen share using think-aloud protocol
Sample:
                                                                                                                                                            * 8–12 participants per prioritized flow
Tasks:
                                                                                                                                                            * Complete activation from start to finish
                                                                                                                                                            * Set up service (port-in or new number)
                                                                                                                                                            * Confirm successful activation
Metrics Collected:
                                                                                                                                                            * Task success rate
                                                                                                                                                            * Time on task
                                                                                                                                                            * Error rate
                                                                                                                                                            * Assistance required
Activation Flow Analysis
Given multiple activation pathways, the following approach will be used:
Step 1: Map All Flows
                                                                                                                                                            * eSIM vs physical SIM
                                                                                                                                                            * Port-in vs new number
                                                                                                                                                            * App vs web activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Unified vs legacy flows
Step 2: Prioritize Flows
Based on:
                                                                                                                                                            * Volume of users
                                                                                                                                                            * Failure/drop-off rates
                                                                                                                                                            * Business impact
Step 3: Focus Testing
                                                                                                                                                            * Conduct deep dives on top 2–3 highest-impact flows
                                                                                                                                                            * Avoid spreading research across low-impact paths
Unified Activation Evaluation
The Unified Activation experience will be evaluated using a before vs after comparison:
Metrics to Compare
                                                                                                                                                            * Activation success rate
                                                                                                                                                            * Step-level drop-offs
                                                                                                                                                            * Time to activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Support contact rates
Evaluation Objective
Determine whether Unified Activation:
                                                                                                                                                            * Reduced friction
                                                                                                                                                            * Shifted friction to different steps
                                                                                                                                                            * Introduced new usability issues
Synthesis Framework
Findings will be categorized into three key areas:
1. UX Friction
                                                                                                                                                            * Confusing flows or navigation
                                                                                                                                                            * Poor visibility of system status
                                                                                                                                                            * Inefficient or redundant steps
2. Cognitive Gaps
                                                                                                                                                            * Misinterpretation of instructions
                                                                                                                                                            * Incorrect assumptions about process steps
                                                                                                                                                            * Mismatch between expectations and outcomes
3. Technical/System Issues
                                                                                                                                                            * Porting failures or delays
                                                                                                                                                            * SIM/eSIM errors
                                                                                                                                                            * Backend or system-related disruptions
Deliverables
Core Outputs
                                                                                                                                                            * Activation funnel analysis with drop-off visualization
                                                                                                                                                            * End-to-end activation journey map
                                                                                                                                                            * Before vs after comparison of Unified Activation
                                                                                                                                                            * Pain point prioritization matrix (Impact × Frequency)
Insights
                                                                                                                                                            * Key drivers of activation failure and early churn
                                                                                                                                                            * User expectation gaps and mental models
                                                                                                                                                            * Flow-specific usability issues
Recommendations
                                                                                                                                                            * Prioritized solutions based on:
                                                                                                                                                            * Business impact
                                                                                                                                                            * Implementation effort
                                                                                                                                                            * Time to value
Expected Outcomes
                                                                                                                                                            * Clear understanding of why users fail to activate
                                                                                                                                                            * Quantified impact of activation friction on churn and revenue
                                                                                                                                                            * Validation of whether Unified Activation improved performance
                                                                                                                                                            * Actionable opportunities to:
                                                                                                                                                            * Increase activation success rates
                                                                                                                                                            * Reduce support costs
                                                                                                                                                            * Improve early customer retention
Timeline (Estimated)
                                                                                                                                                            * Phase 1 (Data Analysis): 1–2 weeks
                                                                                                                                                            * Phase 2 (Interviews): 2 weeks
                                                                                                                                                            * Phase 3 (Usability Testing): 2 weeks
                                                                                                                                                            * Synthesis & Reporting: 1–2 weeks
Total: ~6–8 weeks

VAS Enhancements
VAS Enhancements Usability Test
Shirley Xieli - Apr’26
Research Goals 
The primary goal is to evaluate two competing design concepts for the Boost Mobile add-on management experience to identify which interface best empowers users to discover, purchase, and manage their plan enhancements with minimal friction. 
Research Objectives 
                                                                                                                                                            * Assess Information Architecture: Determine if users can successfully distinguish between "Included" features (base plan) and "Add-on" features (purchased extras) across both designs.
                                                                                                                                                            * Evaluate Transactional Clarity: Measure the success rate and perceived ease of purchasing a one-time add-on versus a recurring "bolt-on."
                                                                                                                                                            * Validate Utilization Mental Models: Confirm that users can accurately interpret data/minute usage visualizations and identify when they need to "refill" or "top-up."
                                                                                                                                                            * Measure Management Efficiency: Quantify the user's ability to locate and execute the "Cancel" function for active recurring services to ensure transparency and trust.
                                                                                                                                                            * Determine Preference & Sentiment: Capture qualitative feedback on which design feels more "intuitive" and "trustworthy" for managing financial transactions.

                                                                                                                                                            * Users who have previously purchased extras, such as global roaming, data hotspot, global talk & text.

Understanding Purchase Consideration
Understanding Purchase Consideration for Boost Mobile
Sphoorthy Masa Oct’25
1. Background
Boost Mobile is experiencing a high volume(assumption) of website traffic that does not convert into sales. Despite offering a wide variety of competitive prepaid, postpaid, single-line, and multi-line plans, a significant number of potential customers abandon the site without making a purchase. This research plan aims to uncover the critical drivers behind this behavior by evaluating brand perception, the competitive landscape, and the on-site user experience. The insights gathered will inform strategic decisions to improve conversion rates and customer acquisition.
2. Research Goals & Objectives
The primary goal of this research is to understand the perceptions, behaviors, and on-site barriers that prevent potential customers from purchasing a Boost Mobile plan.
Primary Objectives:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Brand Perception & Credibility:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Understand the current perception of the Boost Mobile brand among non-customers.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Identify the key attributes users associate with a "credible" mobile service provider.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Determine if Boost Mobile is perceived as lacking credibility and, if so, why.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Competitive Landscape:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Benchmark the Boost Mobile homepage and initial offer presentation against key competitors (Mint Mobile, Metro by T-Mobile, Cricket Wireless, Consumer Cellular).
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Understand Boost's perceived strengths and weaknesses relative to these competitors.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Shopping Behavior & Decision Making:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Uncover the typical research and decision-making process for users shopping for a new mobile provider.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Identify the most critical factors beyond price (e.g., network reliability, customer service, device selection) that influence their choice.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Pinpoint specific "red flags" or sources of hesitation that deter users during their research.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * On-Site Experience & Usability:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Evaluate the clarity, findability, and appeal of plans and offers on the Boost Mobile website.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Identify specific points of friction, confusion, or missing information on the homepage and in the plan selection flow.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Assess why low-commitment trial offers may not be effectively converting interested users.
3. Methodology
This study will employ a two-part, mixed-method approach to gather both quantitative and qualitative data.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              1. Quantitative Survey: A survey will be deployed to a broad audience to measure and quantify user attitudes, existing brand perceptions, and general mobile service shopping behaviors. This will provide a baseline understanding of the market and our target users' priorities.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              2. Unmoderated Usability Testing: In parallel to the survey, an unmoderated usability test will be launched where participants will be asked to complete tasks on the Boost Mobile website and several competitor sites. This will allow us to observe user behavior directly, understand the context behind their actions, and identify specific usability issues and sources of confusion in the user interface.
4. Participants
We will recruit n=1000 participants for the survey and n=15 participants for the unmoderated usability test.
Participant Criteria:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Age: ⅚ user segments
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Location: United States
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Currently use a prepaid or no-contract mobile service provider.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Are not currently a Boost Mobile customer.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Have either actively researched new mobile plans in the past 3 months or are planning to in the next 2 months.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A mix of users whose current providers are Mint Mobile, Metro by T-Mobile, Cricket Wireless, and Consumer Cellular.
5. Research Questions
This study aims to answer the following high-level questions:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * What are the primary drivers and barriers influencing a potential customer's decision to purchase a Boost Mobile plan?
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * How does the perception of Boost Mobile's credibility and value proposition compare to that of its key competitors?
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * What are the most significant pain points in the online discovery and plan evaluation journey on the Boost Mobile website?
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * What critical information or features are users missing that leads them to abandon the purchase process?

Research Goals
The goal of this survey is to understand which Certified Pre-Owned device users would choose when comparing price, device age, model tier, monthly financing, and service plan costs.
The survey will also evaluate whether users prefer a 12-month or 24-month financing option, and whether users are more likely to choose a newer CPO phone over an older one even when the older phone is cheaper.
Research Objectives
Objective 1: Understand device preference
Identify which device users are most likely to choose among:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * iPhone 13 CPO
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * iPhone 14 CPO
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * iPhone 14 Pro / Pro Max CPO
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra CPO
Objective 2: Test the “newer phone” hypothesis
Understand whether users prefer a newer or higher-tier phone over an older CPO model, even if the older model has a lower monthly price.
Objective 3: Understand CPO hesitation
Identify what concerns users have when buying a Certified Pre-Owned phone, especially older models.
Objective 4: Understand 12-month vs. 24-month plan preference
Determine whether users prefer paying less per month over 24 months or finishing payments sooner over 12 months.
Objective 5: Understand service plan impact
Evaluate whether users are more likely to choose a phone when the monthly cost includes the unlimited plan.
Objective 6: Identify the final preferred phone and plan combination
Ask users to select the exact phone and plan they would choose from all available options.
