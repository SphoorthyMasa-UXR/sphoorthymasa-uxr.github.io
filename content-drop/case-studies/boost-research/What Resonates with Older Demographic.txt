Q. In a few words, please describe how your perception of Boost Mobile has changed, if at all, since becoming a customer.
Customers offer mixed reviews across price, coverage and customer service.
Theme 1: Price & Value Recognition
Respondents are highly price sensitive. Some stay with Boost because of affordability, others feel misled by promotions or disappointed by data limitations.

“I am simple. I enjoy paying very little for a very straightforward and very reliable service… Your price lock guarantee is a HUGE selling factor.”

“I’m getting the same service [as Verizon] for 25% of the price.”

“Coverage is decent, pricing is great.”

“The ad advertised $10 per month for two months … but on checking … it says I have to pay $25 which is not what was promised.”

“Price seems misleading. Price is not good for the service. Not realistic and not a good value for the service received.”
Theme 2: Customer Service & Trust
Customer service drives some mixed sentiment, with complaints about dishonored promotions, difficulty with canceling service, and poor interactions.


“The only cell phone company I can actually get ahold of a live agent keeps me coming back.”

“Customer Service is far superior to most other carriers, Especially AT&T.”

“Boost does not adhere to promotional offers. You call and they have NO IDEA of the promotion you signed up for!”

“I quit Boost after first month. Boost promised a rate for 3 months and then did not honor that rate.”

“Boost was unable to complete my service and did not refund my money paid out!”

“Customer service is helpful sometimes, though I've had horrible service in an actual store.”
Theme 3: Coverage & Service Quality
Some praise coverage as better than expected, while others complain about weak service, dead spots, or technical issues.


“Very good coverage, excellent pricing, overall excellent service.”

“Besides price, coverage is better than what I had with Verizon in my area.”

“The signal comes and goes so I probably will look for something else for next month.”

“Coverage is worse than expected. 5G is claimed, but I have not yet been able to connect using it.”

“Thought coverage would be better in our area but unfortunately it is not good.”

“While I appreciate the cost savings, I barely get phone service … Most people who call me say they didn’t hear me speak.”

Next Steps
1. Redesign Core Landing Pages - Link to previous survey insights 
Pricing alignment: Match ad and landing page prices, clarify store vs online differences, and align cart pricing with expectations.
Clear offer positioning: Lead with $25/month, frame $10/month as a short-term savings, reduce fine-print confusion.
Transparency: Add plan details (data caps, device requirements, why is the price low) above the fold, provide off-ramps for more deals without disrupting checkout.
2. Map Survey Responses to CI Segmentation - Estimated Timeline: Sept 15
Connect survey findings to the existing CI bubble chart.
Identify projected high-potential target areas.
3. Target Priority Segments
Share target audience insights with Ovative.
Focus paid efforts on the highest-conversion customer segments.

Insights (Perceived Boost Customers)
Sign-ups were primarily motivated by perceived value and plan affordability rather than specific ad or landing page details.
Price sensitivity came through strongly, with repeated mentions of good prices, cheap plans, and attractive deals.
The ad and landing page captured attention, but clarity of information still did not stand out as a driver.
Younger users (<55) tended to mention curiosity and design appeal
Older users highlighted trust in service quality and reliability.
