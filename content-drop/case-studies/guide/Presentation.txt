Research Questions, Key Insights, & Impact
Executive Summary
Problem: Nearly half of guide sessions end without playback, revealing hidden usability barriers that impact engagement and revenue.
Research Questions:
Why do so many sessions stall before playback?
How might we improve guide usability to reduce drop-offs and help users launch content faster?
Key Pain Points Identified:
Excessive scrolling to find content
Channel clutter causing confusion
Low usage of filters and favorites that could streamline navigation
Impact:
Design iterations driven by research insights significantly enhanced guide efficiency, reduced user drop-offs, and unlocked an estimated ~$1M in incremental revenue potential.

Mixed Channel Listings Create Confusion and Frustration

Users struggle to find desired content due to long, cluttered channel lists.
Users spend excessive time scrolling through irrelevant content.
Leads to cognitive overload and fatigue.
Users want:
The ability to hide channels they never watch.
An easy way to show/hide hidden channels when desired.

Research Suggestions

I based my research suggestions on impact vs effort matrix

On Boarding

Clearly communicate upcoming guide changes at first use to set expectations
Highlight new controls and what users can do differently in the updated guide
Explain the value and outcomes of each feature, not just the feature itself
Focus messaging on how the changes improve navigation and reduce clutter


(Ex: Better channel grouping + Browse through categories effortlessly.)
18

First time users + Favoriting

Introduce an immediate entry point for first-time users to begin favoriting channels
Encourage early engagement with the favorites feature to increase time-to-video and overall viewing duration
Implement a 30-day re-engagement banner for users who have not favorited channels
Offer an auto-favorite option (e.g., top six most-watched channels) to reduce friction and jumpstart personalization

19

Metadata

Enhance content metadata with richer artwork, descriptions, and additional tags
Enable users to better understand content at a glance and make more confident viewing decisions
Reduce hesitation and comparison time by clearly signaling what each title offers
Support faster content selection by strengthening the connection between visual cues and content expectations

20

Contextual Side Drawers

Use side drawers to surface additional information without disrupting the main experience
Provide quick access to secondary actions and controls within context
Enable personalization management directly from the guide
Balance added functionality with a clean, simple interaction model

21

Personalization

Give users control over how their guide is organized and displayed
Enable personalization through favorite channel management
Allow channel sorting by categories or A–Z
Let users choose which channels are displayed or hidden
Support collapsing and expanding channel groups to reduce clutter and improve scannability
22

Decluttering Free Stream

Reduce guide clutter caused by the addition of FreeStream channels
Improve navigation speed and ease of browsing within the guide
Allow users to show or hide FreeStream channels at the category level
Maintain access to FreeStream content while giving users control over visibility
