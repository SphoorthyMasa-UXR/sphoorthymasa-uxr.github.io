Filters Are Invisible

72% used default "ALL" view. Only 9% used Favorites, yet those sessions had 63.9% playback (8.5% higher).

Clutter Overwhelms

Users described "scrolling for eternity." Cognitive overload drives exits within 20 seconds.

Fear Blocks Personalization

Users avoid changing settings because they fear disrupting their workflow.

What shipped

Expanded filter segmentation (+39% usage, ~$2.1M/yr), quick channel favoriting (+62% engagement), guide decluttering (-18% browsing time, ~$2.8M/yr), and onboarding that reduced CX tickets by 31%.

Research Objectives

  • Identify user frustrations, behaviors, and unmet needs in the current guide (Sling TV and Dish TV)
  • Uncover platform-specific and shared pain points to guide unification efforts
  • Investigate personalization and accessibility opportunities to enhance feature adoption
  • Benchmark against competitors to evaluate trends and best practices
  • Deliver actionable design and research recommendations that balance user needs and business goals

Participants & Segments

  • DISH TV Subscribers (65+): remote-based users who tolerate outdated UI but struggle with hidden features
  • Sling TV Subscribers (~35): tech-savvy, expect fast intuitive interfaces, seek quick content access
  • Shared behaviors: watch 3+ hrs/day, use guide 2+ times daily, mixed familiarity with sub-features

Methodology

  • Stakeholder interviews to align on business goals (reduce CX complaints 12%, improve task success 90%)
  • Quantitative data analysis: 1.73M Sling accounts, 2.58M DISH households behavioral data
  • Generative user interviews with task-based scenarios targeting identified friction points
  • KJ analysis (FigJam) for synthesis and insight mapping
  • Collaborative ideation with design, product, and engineering
  • Unmoderated usability testing (UserZoom) with UMUX-Lite scoring per sub-feature

Key Quantitative Insights

  • Sling: 79% of active users used the guide, but only 56% led to playback
  • DISH: 86% used guide, only 48% led to playback
  • 73% of Sling sessions used default "ALL" filter. Only 12% switched filters
  • 44% of Sling guide sessions ended without playback, mostly under 20 seconds
  • DISH: 46.4% relied on vertical navigation out of habit, 15.3% exited with no interaction

Actionable Recommendations Delivered

  • Focus on learning curve: gradual transitions, reversible changes, user-friendly functionalities
  • Intuitive channel lineup: populate wanted channels first, add scroll indicators, filter for hidden channels
  • Balance customizable and standard filters across platforms
  • Educate users on Favorites for quicker content launch (only 9% used it but 63.9% playback rate)
  • Prioritize accessibility: WCAG compliance, color contrast, font size optimization

H1 DEBUNKED

"Older users who struggle with tech will prefer this." Correlation ≈ 0. Interest driven by convenience, not inability. t=7.22, p<0.001

H2 NOT SUPPORTED

"Rural users prefer it due to no stores." Urban trended higher. p=0.12, not significant.

H3 CONFIRMED

Active switchers (18-34) love it. Highest interest (47%), switch intent (29%), NPS (7.7/10).

Impact

Debunked the core assumption. Recommended 70%+ budget reallocation to 18-44 for 3-5x ROI. Winning message: "Free delivery + free setup. Just pick a time."

Research Objectives

  • Validate or debunk 4 leadership hypotheses about Store-to-Door service demand
  • Identify the actual target demographic with highest conversion potential
  • Determine optimal messaging and positioning for go-to-market
  • Provide data-backed budget allocation recommendation to VP leadership

Methodology & Execution

  • Solo researcher: designed hypotheses, survey instrument, quota strategy
  • Qualtrics survey, n=539, fielded in 2 days with demographic quotas
  • Statistical analysis: t-tests, correlation analysis, regression, cross-tabulation
  • Segmentation by age, geography (rural/suburban/urban), tech comfort, switching intent
  • NPS scoring by segment, willingness-to-pay analysis

Key Statistical Findings

  • H1: No correlation between tech difficulty and service interest (r≈0, t=7.22, p<0.001 for convenience driver)
  • H2: Rural vs urban preference not significant (p=0.12), urban trended higher
  • H3: 18-34 segment showed 47% interest vs 23% for 55+
  • H4: "Free" messaging outperformed "premium service" by 2.3x in click-through intent
  • Conversion potential: 3-5x higher ROI targeting 18-44 vs 55+

Deliverables

  • VP-level presentation with statistical evidence for budget reallocation
  • Segment-specific messaging recommendations with confidence intervals
  • Alternative service design (video-call setup) for 55+ segment that tested well
  • Go-to-market framework with phased rollout recommendation

Users Don't Know What IMEI Is

Low baseline awareness. Cognitive friction stops users before they even try.

Trust > Instructions

56.4% would enter IMEI if "why it's needed" is explained. The barrier isn't how, it's why.

Help Link Breaks Flow

IMEI help redirects to generic BYOD page. Users exit the funnel and never return.

Impact

The barrier wasn't usability, it was understanding and trust. Solution: education-first messaging + inline help + trust indicators. Now scaling to all IMEI-dependent flows.

Research Objectives

  • Identify why 4,363 users/month drop off at IMEI entry despite a recent redesign
  • Determine if the barrier is awareness, trust, usability, or a combination
  • Provide actionable recommendations within 3 business days

Methodology & Execution

  • Parallel mixed-methods: quantitative survey (n=1,000 via Qualtrics) + moderated usability testing (n=15)
  • Survey segmented by: new vs returning users, BYOD vs upgrade, age, tech comfort
  • Usability sessions: think-aloud protocol, task completion at IMEI entry step
  • Analytics cross-reference: funnel data, session recordings (Quantum Metric), CX ticket analysis
  • Total turnaround: 3 days from brief to presentation-ready recommendations

Deeper Insights

  • 67% of drop-off users had never heard of "IMEI" before reaching this page
  • 56.4% said they'd enter it if the page explained why it's needed (trust framing)
  • Help link took users to a generic BYOD page with 12% return rate (funnel break)
  • Users who saw a "why we need this" tooltip had 3x higher completion rate in usability
  • Mobile users had 2.1x higher drop-off than desktop (screen real estate for help content)

Recommendations Delivered

  • Education-first: explain "why" before asking "what" at every IMEI entry point
  • Inline contextual help (no page navigation away from funnel)
  • Trust indicators: lock icon, privacy reassurance, "we never store this"
  • Mobile-optimized layout with expandable help drawer
  • Partnered with Product and E-commerce to implement across all IMEI-dependent flows
